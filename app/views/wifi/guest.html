Using user.wifi

user.wifi is the new shared Wi-Fi system for government buildings. It's currently in beta in the following buildings:

  • Aviation House
  • Home Office
  • Civil Service Live venues around the country

How to connect

If you're in one of the places listed above, your device should be picking up user.wifi.

To receive your username and password, text "wifi" to 82228.

Once you agree to the Terms & Conditions and enter your credentials into your device, you will be connected in most user.wifi enabled buildings.

Verifying your account

Some buildings require you to verify your account for security purposes.

You can do this by proving that you are in the building at a kiosk. You'll receive instructions on how to do this by text when you enter the building. The verification lasts for 24 hours.

If you're a public sector employee, you can permanently verify yourself. To do this, send an email to enrol@user.wifi.service.gov.uk with the phone number you used to sign up as the subject header.

Troubleshooting

If you're using a government owned device, you should contact the support desk who gave it to you.

If you're using your own device, you should contact the support desk in the building that you're in.

If you can't get in touch with either of the above teams, email support@user.wifi.service.gov.uk with your location, device model, and what went wrong.
